Why We Use Insurance-Approved Locks

Having insurance-approved locks makes your home or office more secure. Locks that comply with the British Standard 3621-2004 have been tested by a governing body and passed specific tests to comply with the British Kitemark Sign. This means that your home or office insurance will be valid if you were broken into through the British Standard Lock. Most insurance companies will ask what locks are on internal doors as well as external doors.

Cylinders in uPVC doors do not need to be British Standard to pass insurance approval. This is because the entire door is insurance approved and can be referred to as a 5-point locking system. However, having a British Standard cylinder fitted to your uPVC door will make the property more secure than a non-British Standard cylinder.

Non Destructive Methods

Being locked out of your home or business premises can be stressful. At Locksmith Direct, we aim to take the stress out of the situation by arriving at our agreed time and using non-destructive methods such as picking the lock instead of drilling it.

Using non-destructive methods makes the jobs cheaper as we will not have to replace the lock and causes less noise and hassle.

If you have lost your keys we recommend getting the locks changed. We will still use non-destructive methods even if the keys have been lost or stolen.
